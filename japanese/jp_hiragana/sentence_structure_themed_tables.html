Japanese Sentence Structure — Themed Tables

Each table shows English sentences written in Japanese word order — Topic first, Object second, Verb last. This is the opposite of English which puts the verb in the middle. Reading the English row trains your brain to expect the verb at the end, which is the single most important habit to build in Japanese.

wa  は marks the topic — what the sentence is about  ·  Ken wa = "as for Ken..."
wo  を marks the object — the thing receiving the action  ·  gohan wo = "rice [is what I eat]"
ni  に marks a person receiving something, or a destination  ·  Yuki ni = "to Yuki"
de  で marks the location where an action happens  ·  kouen de = "at the park"
ka  か added to the end of a verb to make a question — no word order change needed  ·  tabemasu ka? = "do you eat?"
